Improv Guide — UCB Harold
A personal reference guide for long-form improv, built around the UCB (Upright Citizens Brigade) Harold structure.
I started going to improv classes at Improv Iceland 3 years ago and just finished their Improv 301 course, where we learned The Harold for the first time. This guide is what I wish I'd had from the beginning — a clear, visual reference for everything from the basics to the stuff that only clicks after you've done it wrong a few times.
What's in it

The Harold — full structure explained with a beat map, the opening, all three beats, and the group games
Mapping & Beat transitions — how to track scenes from the back wall and what Beat 2 actually means (not just "the same scene again")
Beginner guide — plain language explanation of the most common mistakes, including the Beat 1 → Beat 2 problem
The three types of Beat 2 — time dash, analogous, and tangential, with examples
Full worked example — a complete Harold traced from suggestion through Beat 3
Glossary — UCB terms including game of the scene, half game, mapping, twisted logic, pop a wheelie, and more

Tips & Tricks
